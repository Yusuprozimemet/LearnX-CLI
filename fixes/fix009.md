# fix009 — Per-unit loudnorm normalization corrupted audio duration

**Date:** 2026-05-09

## Symptom

Full session audio mean volume was −29.7 dB (nearly inaudible). Individual unit
MP4s were fine (−17 dB) but after `_normalize_audio` the video stream extended
from 224 s to 276 s, and the full-session concat was dominated by silence.

## Root cause

`_normalize_audio` ran:

```
ffmpeg -i unit_01.mp4 -af loudnorm=... unit_01_norm.mp4
```

The video stream in the unit MP4 consists of **3–4 large keyframes** spaced
80–120 s apart (PNG slides held via the concat demuxer). The EBU R128
integration window in single-pass `loudnorm` cannot handle inter-frame gaps of
this size; it produced a 52-second extension of the video stream while the audio
stayed at 207 s. The resulting 69 s of silence after the audio ended dragged the
session mean volume down to −30 dB.

Attempted intermediate fixes (separating audio normalization into a two-step
process using an ADTS `.aac` intermediary) also failed: the ADTS container does
not store accurate duration metadata, causing the mux step to produce incorrect
results.

## Files changed

- `tutor/visual/video_assembler.py`

## What changed

1. Removed the separate `_normalize_audio` call from the per-unit loop in
   `assemble_session()`. Unit MP4s are appended directly without normalization.

2. Added `-af volume=5dB` to `_build_unit_video()` so the 5 dB boost is applied
   **at encode time**, alongside the MP3 audio. The raw TTS MP3s are at ≈ −21 dB
   mean; +5 dB yields ≈ −16 dB in the final video — matching the loudnorm target
   without touching the video stream at all.

Result: full session at −17.8 dB mean, 0.0 dB max.
