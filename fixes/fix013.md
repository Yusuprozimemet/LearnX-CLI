# fix013 — Video audio completely silent due to sample-rate mismatch in concat

**Date:** 2026-05-09

## Symptom

Generated `full_session.mp4` had no audible voice even though the source
`tutorial.mp3` played correctly. `ffprobe` on the final video reported
`sr=44100 ch=2` (stereo) across the full 623-second output, but unit MP4s
were encoded at `sr=24000 ch=1` (mono). Players decoded the mixed stream
as silence after the 4-second title card.

## Root causes

### 1. Audio stream format mismatch → broken concat stream

`_build_unit_video()` in `tutor/visual/video_assembler.py` encoded audio
at whatever sample rate/channels the input TTS MP3 had (Google TTS:
**24000 Hz mono**). The title card used `anullsrc=channel_layout=stereo:
sample_rate=44100` → **44100 Hz stereo**.

`_concat_unit_videos()` used `-c copy` to join all segments. The MP4
container adopts the codec parameters of the first segment (44100 Hz
stereo). Every subsequent frame (24000 Hz mono) is stored with mismatched
parameters. Most players read the header, attempt to decode mono-24000 kHz
frames as stereo-44100 kHz, and produce silence.

### 2. Slide durations inflated beyond actual MP3 duration

`compute_slide_timings()` calculated `unit_end` from the next unit's hook
offset in `beat_map`. Those offsets come from `get_line_start_offsets()`
which adds `SILENCE_TURN_MS` (500 ms) between every dialogue turn and
`SILENCE_UNIT_MS` (1200 ms) between units. For a unit with ~30 lines, this
inflates the "end" by ~15 s. The concat slides for unit 1 totalled 224 s
but the actual MP3 was 207 s → 17 s of black silence at the end of each
unit video.

## Files changed

- `tutor/visual/video_assembler.py` — `_build_unit_video()`
- `tutor/visual/beat_timer.py` — `compute_slide_timings()`

## What changed

### video_assembler.py — `_build_unit_video()`

Added three arguments to the ffmpeg command:
- `-map 0:v:0 -map 1:a:0` — explicit stream selection (video from concat
  input, audio from MP3 input); removes ambiguity when ffmpeg auto-selects.
- `-ar 44100 -ac 2` — normalises all unit video audio to 44100 Hz stereo,
  matching the title and outro cards so `-c copy` concat produces a
  consistent stream throughout.
- `-shortest` — stops encoding when the shorter stream ends, preventing an
  infinite loop when the concat demuxer's last repeated frame has no
  explicit duration.

### beat_timer.py — `compute_slide_timings()`

`unit_end` is now taken from the **actual cumulative MP3 duration** rather
than from the next unit's beat-map hook offset. A pre-computed dict maps
each unit index to its cumulative audio end:

```python
unit_audio_end: dict[int, float] = {}
_cursor = 0.0
for _ui, _dur in enumerate(unit_durations_s, start=1):
    _cursor += _dur
    unit_audio_end[_ui] = _cursor

unit_end = unit_audio_end.get(unit_idx, total_audio_end)
```

Result: total slide duration for each unit now equals the actual MP3
duration, eliminating the trailing silence.
